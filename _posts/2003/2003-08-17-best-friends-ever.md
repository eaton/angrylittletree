So Saturday evening I wander over to <b><a href="http://rdecker.livejournal.com">rdecker</a>'s</b> place for a Fusion meeting, a little tired and zoned after a strange week and a strange start to the weekend. I'm listening to Orbital and thinking of getting together with someone after the meeting and all. I buzz in at the door, wander into the apartment, and experience a moment of abject confusion. There are people that don't normally come to the planning meetings in her apartment. They are milling around. There are balloons. And all the girls are screaming.

It's a surprise party.

<a href="http://rdecker.livejournal.com">rdecker</a>, that crafty crafty lass, had orchestrated a surprise party and,<a href="http://blaster151.livejournal.com">blaster151</a>, <a href="http://jefner1.livejournal.com">jefner1</a>, <a href="http://js0n.livejournal.com">js0n</a>, Jasmine, Sarah, and Carrie all showed up. Throughout the night others who weren't able to make it called to wish me a happy birthday. I was overwhelmed. Baffled and disoriented, even!

I was moved.

Even <a href="http://texasjeff.livejournal.com">texasjeff</a> had sent a gift to be opened -- I'm now the proud owner of a <a href="http://www.homestarrunner.com" target="_blank">Strongbad</a> t-shirt, among other swanky gifts. We hung out, we laughed, we had key lime pie, we took oodles of photos with my new 20mm wide angle lens. I have the best friends ever -- it's as simple as that. To those who are reading this, thank you. You're a blessing to me that words can't even begin to describe. Thanks for being you, each and every one of you, and thank you for knowing me.

The pictures aren't all mine -- <a href="http://blaster151.livejournal.com">blaster151</a> and <a href="http://jefner1.livejournal.com">jefner1</a> got a chance to play around with the camera too. So this batch of photos is a purely social one...
